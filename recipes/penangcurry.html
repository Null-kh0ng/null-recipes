Penang Curry

Also spelled “phanaeng,” penang curry is a red curry originally from Thailand named for the island of Penang. It's commonly made with chicken or beef and is eaten with rice with a garnish of chopped lime leaves and Thai basil

Ingredients

Gather the ingredients below and find your tastebuds in a tropical paradise!

  • diced chicken
  • vegetable oil
  • sliced red pepper
  • chili peppers
  • garlic
  • curry paste
  • coconut milk
  • brown sugar
  • fish sauce
  • lime juice

Directions

  1. Start with browning the chicken in some vegetable oil on all sides. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Add sliced red pepper and cook until softened (about 5 minutes).
  3. Add the garlic and the chili peppers and stir for about 30 seconds.
  4. Add the chicken back to the pan. Add the curry paste and brown sugar. Fry until fragrant.
  5. Stir in the coconut milk. Stir the fish sauce, lime juice, and zest into the mixture.
  6. Pour over rice and serve!
